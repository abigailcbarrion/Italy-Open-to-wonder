La dolce vita

The Italian people live by the concept of "la dolce vita" which means “the sweet life” or “the good life.”

Celebrating Life in Italy

Italy is a country that comes alive with vibrant festivals and cherished holidays. Each one brimming with history, culture, and unforgettable experiences. You can find the spirit of celebration into the Italian life. Either you’re exploring the winding streets of a historic city or enjoying the serene landscapes of the countryside.

Carnevale di Venezia

Carnavale is a traditional festival in Venice held every year before Lent. People wear elaborate masks and costumes, and the city hosts parades, music, and street performances. It is famous worldwide for its colorful and artistic celebrations.

Palio di Siena

The Palio is a historic horse race held twice a year in Siena. Different city districts compete around the Piazza del Campo in a fast, exciting race. The event also includes medieval pageantry and local traditions that attract many visitors.

Slow Living in Italy

An important part of Italian culture is their commitment to enjoying a slower pace of life. From taking a proper lunch break to their ritual aperitivo, Italians take rest, recovery, and social time very seriously.

Italian Menu

Italian food is one of the most popular cuisines in America, while pizza and pasta rank among the world's favorite dishes. Though Italian fare varies across the country's 20 geographical regions and throughout American interpretations, the myriad combinations of iconic ingredients like garlic, olive oil, rich tomatoes, fresh cheeses, pasta, and wine are infinitely compelling.
